**[Slide 1: 开头 / Introduction]** Hello everyone. Data volume grows every single day. Today, I will discuss the evolution of database management systems. We are living in an era of big data and cloud computing. This era brings massive amounts of diverse and fast-moving data. Because of this, the way databases work is changing completely.

**[Slide 2: 目录 / Table of Contents]** In this presentation, I will skip the deep technical details. Instead, I will focus on two main lifelines for any business. These are cost and security. We will look at their past limits, their present solutions, and their future directions. Let us begin with the cost.

**[Slide 3: 中间段落 1 (成本的过去) / Body 1]** First, let us look at the past of database costs. Companies had to spend a lot of money upfront on physical equipment. To build a data storage center, businesses bought expensive, dedicated servers. The main problem was resource waste. Computing power and storage space were strictly tied together. Companies had to buy extra resources just to handle rare moments of high user traffic. During normal days, these extra resources simply sat idle. The cost benefits were basically zero or even negative. In short, this old system wasted money and hurt growing businesses.

**[Slide 4: 中间段落 2 (成本的现在与未来) / Body 2]** Now, let us look at the present and future of database costs. Today, cloud databases are actively solving this waste problem. They change high upfront costs into precise daily operating expenses right now. As Skourletopoulos noted in 2017, cloud computing makes 'Big Data as a Service' a reality today. Companies no longer buy physical hardware. They just pay for exactly what they use. Furthermore, looking to the latest technology, modern systems separate the computing parts from the storage parts. According to Dong in 2024, modern architecture brings billing down to the exact search level. If users are not searching for data, the system scales down automatically. Therefore, current and future database costs are highly flexible and efficient.

**[Slide 5: 中间段落 3 (安全的过去) / Body 3]** Let us move on to the past of database security. Security relied heavily on physical barriers. It used static, unchanging access controls. The old model used firewalls and private company networks to stop outside attacks. However, this static system had a major flaw. It could not handle large, fast, and complex data. Today, data comes from many sources, like text, images, and videos. Bertino highlighted in 2015 that old access controls struggle with this variety. These old rules are simply too weak now. As a result, just building a physical wall is no longer a safe strategy.

**[Slide 6: 中间段落 4 (安全的现在与未来) / Body 4]** Finally, let us look at the present and future of database security. Today, the cloud creates new challenges, but also better solutions. For instance, multiple different companies currently share the same physical server. Modern databases must use digital barriers today to stop data leaks between these different users. To achieve this, they rely on advanced encryption and automatic permission settings. As Qi pointed out in 2020, systems must automatically manage hundreds of security rules. Looking further ahead, the best future protection is hardware-based data protection. This allows the database to process data while it is still locked and encrypted. Even the cloud provider cannot see the readable data. Consequently, modern databases offer deep security for absolute privacy now and in the future.

**[Slide 7: 结尾 / Conclusion]** To sum up, cloud computing has transformed database systems. They are no longer expensive, stiff assets protected by basic firewalls. Instead, they are flexible, efficient, and highly secure digital tools. In the past, you had to choose between low cost and high security. Today, modern designs break this rule. Companies can now get top-level security for a very low price. Looking ahead, the 5G era will push data volumes to massive new levels. Mastering these modern cloud databases is crucial. It is the key for businesses to control costs, protect privacy, and succeed today and in the future.
